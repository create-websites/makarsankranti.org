Uttarayan vs Makar Sankranti

What’s the difference? Meaning, dates, and how each is celebrated—especially in Gujarat.

Quick answer (Uttarayan vs Makar Sankranti)

Makar Sankranti is the widely used pan-India festival name around mid-January. It is associated with the sun’s transition and is celebrated with regional traditions, food, and rituals.

Uttarayan is the term most strongly associated with Gujarat’s celebration style— especially the famous rooftop kite flying. In everyday usage, many people say “Uttarayan” when they mean the kite festival days around Makar Sankranti.

In simple words: Makar Sankranti = the festival name, and Uttarayan = the Gujarat-style celebration identity (kite festival focus) that happens at the same time.

Why Gujarat calls it “Uttarayan”

In Gujarat, the mid-January festival is deeply tied to kite flying—terraces fill with families, friends, music, and snacks. Because the kite festival identity is so strong, people commonly say “Uttarayan” to describe the entire celebration experience.

Rooftop culture

Families spend the day on terraces with kites, reels, and snacks.

Friendly competition

Patang battles, cheering, and city-wide excitement.

Markets & prep

Kite markets, manja reels, and festival foods become part of the ritual.

FAQ

Is Uttarayan only celebrated in Gujarat?

The term “Uttarayan” is most strongly used in Gujarat. Other regions celebrate the same mid-January festival with different names and traditions.

Do Uttarayan and Makar Sankranti fall on the same day?

They usually refer to the same time window around mid-January. In Gujarat, “Uttarayan” often covers the celebration days around Sankranti.
