Why these are the top Uttarayan recipes

During Uttarayan, families gather on terraces for kite flying and winter feasts. Two foods are searched again and again: the hearty undhiyu recipe (a Gujarati winter specialty) and the sweet til chikki makar sankranti (sesame brittle made with jaggery).

Winter comfort

Seasonal ingredients and warm, filling meals.

Sharing culture

Sweets symbolize sweetness and goodwill.

Easy planning

Make-ahead snacks for all-day kite flying.

Undhiyu Recipe (Traditional Gujarati)

Prep
30 min
Cook
60 min
Serves
4

Ingredients (core)

  • Mixed winter vegetables (surti papdi, brinjal, potato, sweet potato, etc.)
  • Coconut (grated), coriander, green garlic (optional)
  • Peanuts + sesame
  • Spices: turmeric, chili, coriander powder, garam masala
  • Oil, salt, lemon

Copied!

Steps

  1. Prep vegetables: wash/cut vegetables; keep brinjals in water.
  2. Make masala: mix coconut, coriander, peanuts, sesame and spices.
  3. Stuff & layer: stuff brinjals/papdi and layer everything with masala.
  4. Slow cook: cover and cook on low heat; stir gently.
  5. Finish: adjust salt, add lemon, garnish and serve hot.

Tip

Low heat and gentle mixing helps keep vegetables intact and flavors balanced.

Til Chikki (Makar Sankranti)

Prep
10 min
Cook
15 min
Makes
~18 pieces

Ingredients

  • Sesame seeds (til)
  • Jaggery (gud)
  • Ghee (optional)
  • Cardamom (optional)
  • Roasted peanuts (optional)

Copied!

Steps

  1. Roast til: dry roast sesame on low heat until aromatic.
  2. Melt gud: melt jaggery on low heat; test the set stage in water.
  3. Mix fast: add sesame (and peanuts/cardamom) and stir quickly.
  4. Spread & cut: transfer to greased surface; cut while warm.
  5. Cool: cool fully, then store airtight for crispness.

Tip

Work quickly after mixing jaggery and sesame—chikki sets fast.
